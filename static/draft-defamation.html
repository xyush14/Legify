Before you fill — the essentials भरने से पहले — अनिवार्य बातें
An interim injunction is hard to get. Post Bloomberg TV v. Zee Entertainment, (2024) INSC 255, courts apply the three-fold test with reasons and the Bonnard v. Perryman standard — an injunction restraining publication is granted only in exceptional cases where the content is shown to be malicious or palpably false. So plead falsity + malice, not merely that the words are defamatory. अंतरिम निषेधाज्ञा मिलना कठिन है। Bloomberg TV बनाम Zee Entertainment, (2024) INSC 255 के बाद न्यायालय त्रि-आयामी परीक्षण कारण सहित तथा Bonnard v. Perryman मानक लागू करते हैं — प्रकाशन रोकने की निषेधाज्ञा केवल असाधारण मामलों में दी जाती है जहाँ सामग्री दुर्भावनापूर्ण या स्पष्टतः असत्य सिद्ध हो। अतः केवल मानहानिकारक नहीं, बल्कि असत्यता व दुर्भावना का अभिवचन करें।
Elements & facts to collectतत्व व एकत्र करने योग्य तथ्य
  • The statement — the exact defamatory words (verbatim), and their meaning/innuendo.
  • Publication — medium, date, and that a third party saw/heard it (screenshot / clipping / URL).
  • Reference — that it identifies the Plaintiff.
  • Falsity & malice — it is untrue and made without justification.
  • Harm — injury to reputation; any special (pecuniary) loss.
  • Libel vs slander — written/online = libel (per se); spoken = slander (usually needs special damage).
  • कथन — अपमानजनक शब्द यथावत्, तथा उनका अर्थ/लांछन।
  • प्रकाशन — माध्यम, तिथि, तथा किसी तृतीय पक्ष ने देखा/सुना (स्क्रीनशॉट / कतरन / URL)।
  • संदर्भ — कथन वादी की ओर इंगित करता है।
  • असत्यता व दुर्भावना — कथन असत्य एवं बिना औचित्य के है।
  • क्षति — प्रतिष्ठा को हानि; कोई विशेष (धन-संबंधी) हानि।
  • लिबेल बनाम स्लैंडर — लिखित/ऑनलाइन = लिबेल (स्वतः वादयोग्य); मौखिक = स्लैंडर (प्रायः विशेष क्षति आवश्यक)।
Checks, reliefs & authoritiesजाँच, अनुतोष व न्याय-दृष्टांत
  • Limitation — 1 year from publication (Limitation Act 1963, Art. 75 libel / Art. 76 slander). File promptly.
  • Reliefs — damages + permanent injunction + removal/retraction & apology.
  • Court fee — ad valorem on the damages claimed (State schedule).
  • Forum — civil court by pecuniary value; where published/circulated or the Defendant resides.
  • Online — file a §63 BSA (old §65B Evidence Act) certificate for the screenshots.
  • Authorities — R. Rajagopal, (1994) 6 SCC 632; Bloomberg v. Zee, (2024) INSC 255; Bonnard v. Perryman, [1891] 2 Ch 269; Tata Sons v. Greenpeace, 2011 SCC OnLine Del 466.
  • परिसीमा — 1 वर्ष प्रकाशन से (परिसीमा अधिनियम 1963, अनु. 75 लिबेल / अनु. 76 स्लैंडर)। शीघ्र दाखिल करें।
  • अनुतोष — क्षतिपूर्ति + स्थायी निषेधाज्ञा + हटाना/खंडन एवं क्षमायाचना।
  • न्याय शुल्क — दावाकृत क्षतिपूर्ति पर यथामूल्य (राज्य अनुसूची)।
  • फोरम — आर्थिक मूल्य अनुसार सिविल न्यायालय; जहाँ प्रकाशित/प्रसारित या प्रतिवादी निवासी।
  • ऑनलाइन — स्क्रीनशॉट हेतु धारा 63 BSA (पूर्व धारा 65B साक्ष्य अधि.) प्रमाणपत्र लगाएँ।
  • न्याय-दृष्टांत — R. Rajagopal, (1994) 6 SCC 632; Bloomberg v. Zee, (2024) INSC 255; Bonnard v. Perryman, [1891] 2 Ch 269; Tata Sons v. Greenpeace, 2011 SCC OnLine Del 466.

The Plaintiff most respectfully submits as under:—

1. That the Plaintiff is [description of the Plaintiff] and enjoys a good reputation and standing in society and in his/her profession/business.

6. That the said statement is false and untrue, and was made maliciously and without any lawful justification, with intent to harm the Plaintiff's reputation.

7. That by reason of the said publication the Plaintiff has been gravely injured in reputation, exposed to public hatred, ridicule and contempt, and has suffered mental agony and [special/pecuniary loss — loss of business/employment ____], and claims general and special damages of ₹[amount].

8. That the Plaintiff got served upon the Defendant a legal notice dated [date] to retract, apologise and pay damages, which the Defendant has failed to comply with.

9. That unless restrained by a permanent injunction, the Defendant is likely to continue and repeat the said defamatory publication, causing the Plaintiff continuing and irreparable injury for which damages alone are not an adequate remedy.

10. That the cause of action arose on [date] when the statement was published, and on [date] when the notice went uncomplied — at [place], within the jurisdiction of this Hon'ble Court.

11. That this Hon'ble Court has territorial jurisdiction (the matter having been published/circulated, and/or the Defendant residing/working for gain, within its limits) and pecuniary jurisdiction to try the suit.

12. That the suit is within limitation, being filed within one year of the said publication.

13. That for court fee and jurisdiction the suit is valued at ₹[amount], and the requisite ad valorem court fee is paid.

Prayer

It is therefore most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may be pleased to:—

  1. pass a decree for ₹[amount] as damages in favour of the Plaintiff and against the Defendant, with interest under Section 34 CPC;
  2. grant a permanent injunction restraining the Defendant, his agents and representatives, from publishing, repeating or circulating the said or any similar defamatory statement against the Plaintiff, by any medium whatsoever;
  3. grant a mandatory injunction directing the Defendant to remove/retract the said statement and to tender an apology;
  4. award costs of the suit; and
  5. grant any other relief this Hon'ble Court deems fit in the interest of justice.

  5. न्यायहित में अन्य जो भी उचित अनुतोष हो, प्रदान करे।

  • Set out the words verbatim (para 3) — a defamation plaint must reproduce the exact words; do not paraphrase. Plead the innuendo (para 4) if the words are defamatory only by implication.
  • Libel vs slander: written/online = libel, actionable per se; spoken = slander, usually needs special damage (plead the pecuniary loss).
  • Public figure / official: R. Rajagopal v. State of T.N., (1994) 6 SCC 632 — a public official cannot recover for libel touching official conduct unless the statement is false and made with reckless disregard for truth.
  • Attach: the defamatory material (clipping/screenshot with URL & date) + a §63 BSA (§65B Evidence Act) certificate for electronic records; proof of reputation and loss; legal notice + postal proof.
  • Preempt defences: truth/justification, fair comment on a matter of public interest, and privilege — anticipate and answer them.
  • Parallel criminal track: defamation is also an offence under §356 BNS (§499–500 IPC) — a separate complaint may be filed; this pack is the civil remedy.
