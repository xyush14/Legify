How it works

Three modes. The way research actually happens.

01

Describe a situation.

Type the matter in plain English — the way you'd explain it to a colleague over chai. Headnote returns the three most relevant precedents from a curated corpus.

"My client received a cheque dishonour notice but the notice was sent to a wrong address — what's the position on validity of notice?"

Supreme Court · 2007 · (2007) 6 SCC 555

C.C. Alavi Haji v. Palapetty Muhammed

NI Act, S. 138 — General Clauses Act, S. 27 — Service of notice — Returned 'unclaimed' or 'refused' — Service is presumed. (Paras 11–17)

✓ Verified to source
02

Ask a topic.

For doctrinal research — five-fold test for circumstantial evidence, S. 482 quashing on settlement. Headnote returns a topic-organised digest in chambers-notebook form.

"When can a conviction be sustained on circumstantial evidence alone?"

The foundational test

Sharad Birdhichand Sarda, 1984. Five golden principles. Suspicion never substitutes proof. (Para 153)

Acquittal on broken chain

Rajesh v. State of MP, 2023. DNA evidence held suspect; chain incomplete. (Paras 33–34)

Conviction sustained

Manu Sharma, 2010. Ballistics + witness + recovery. (Paras 271–285)

03

Paste a judgment.

Drop full judgment text. Headnote produces lettered headnotes — one per discrete point of law — in journal format, with cases referred and treatment classification.

[paste: full text of any criminal judgment from Indian Kanoon, eCourts, or your file]

Supreme Court · 2025 · 2025 INSC 132

Karuppudayar v. State

(A) SC-ST Act, S. 3(1)(r), (s) — "Public view" requirement — Office with no third party present does not satisfy. (Paras 14–19)

(B) CrPC, S. 482 — Quashing under Bhajan Lal first category. (Paras 17, 21)

✓ Verified · 3 cases referred

Practitioner questions

What lawyers ask first.

Will it hallucinate citations?+

By architecture, no. Every cited case must exist in our corpus; hallucinations are dropped before display. Every paragraph anchor and quoted phrase is verified against source. After February 2026, this is non-negotiable.

How current is the corpus?+

Currently 42 hand-curated landmark Supreme Court judgments. Roadmap: 500 by Q2, 5,000 by Q3 — including post-July-2024 BNS-era rulings. You can request specific cases be added.

Why criminal law only?+

Depth, not breadth. Horizontal AI legal-tech is shallow on every domain. Criminal law has its own taxonomy, statutes, and appellate doctrines. We chose to be world-class on one thing.

Does it work in Hindi?+

Yes — one-click Devanagari translation on every result. Case names, citations, statute references, and paragraph anchors are preserved verbatim in English.

Can I cite Headnote in court?+

You cite the underlying judgment, not Headnote. Every result links to the source paragraph. Treat Headnote as a research assistant, not a citation source.
