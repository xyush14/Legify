The case for this

A criminal-law tool, not a generic AI wrapper.

In India, criminal practice happens at midnight. A junior is handed a brief at 9 p.m., the bail hearing is at 11 a.m., and the cases that matter are buried somewhere between Indian Kanoon, a senior's borrowed SCC login, and the printed Cri.L.J. volume on the chambers shelf. The work is brutal and the tools are wrong.

The legacy databases — SCC Online, Manupatra, AIR — are excellent and unaffordable. Forty thousand a year for a single seat, in a profession where 72% of juniors earn under twenty thousand a month. The new AI tools that arrived after 2023 are cheap and dangerous. In February 2026 the Supreme Court formally ruled that citing AI-generated fake precedents constitutes judicial misconduct — and quietly, every honest practitioner stopped trusting AI summaries.

Two months later, the IPC, the CrPC, and the Evidence Act were replaced by BNS, BNSS, and BSA. Three decades of precedent now reference repealed statutes. The big publishers are still re-indexing.

Headnote is the small, opinionated answer to all three problems at once.

We build only for criminal lawyers. We produce only headnotes — the format that pastes into a written submission, not chatty summaries you have to rewrite. We verify every citation against a real corpus before it reaches you. We map every IPC reference to its BNS successor. And we are free during beta because pricing should not be the reason a junior advocate cannot research.

— Founder, Headnote · Spring 2026

What we make

The output, in three forms.

Three modes that map to how research actually happens — from the moment a brief lands on your desk to the moment a paragraph ends up in a written submission.

I

Describe a situation. Receive cases.

Plain-English query → 3 strongly relevant precedents.

A complaint under S. 138 NI Act has been filed in Delhi where the complainant received the cheque, but the drawee bank dishonoured it in Mumbai. What are the governing precedents on territorial jurisdiction?

Dashrath Rupsingh Rathod v. State of Maharashtra

SC, 2014 · (2014) 9 SCC 129 · 2014 Cri.L.J. 4350

NI Act S. 138 — CrPC S. 177 — Territorial jurisdiction — Return of cheque by drawee bank alone constitutes commission of offence — Situs of trial restricted to where drawee bank is located. (Paras 16, 17)

II

Ask a topic. Receive a digest.

Doctrinal question → chambers-notebook-style digest.

Five-fold test for circumstantial evidence — when can a conviction be sustained on circumstantial evidence alone?

Foundational test

Sharad Birdhichand Sarda v. State of Maharashtra, (1984) 4 SCC 116. Five golden principles (panchsheel): circumstances fully established · consistent only with guilt · conclusive nature · every alternative hypothesis excluded · chain complete. Suspicion alone never substitutes proof. (Para 153)

Acquittal on broken chain

Rajesh v. State of MP, 2023 INSC 839. Death-penalty acquittal where DNA from hair was inherently improbable and police investigation flawed. (Paras 33–34)

III

Paste a judgment. Receive a headnote.

Full judgment text → lettered headnotes for each point of law.

[pasted: full text of Karuppudayar v. State, 2025 INSC 132, on the SC-ST Act "public view" requirement]

(A) SC-ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, Ss. 3(1)(r), 3(1)(s) — Caste-based abuse — "In any place within public view" — Office with no third party present — Public-view requirement is not satisfied where the alleged abuse occurs inside an office with no independent third party present at the time of the incident. (Paras 14–19)

(B) CrPC, 1973, S. 482 — Inherent powers — Quashing — Bhajan Lal first category — Allegations even at face value not constituting offence. (Paras 17, 21)

A pledge

After February 2026, "AI summary" is not a defence in a courtroom.

On 27 February 2026 the Supreme Court formally classified AI-generated fake citations as judicial misconduct. We built our verification architecture before that ruling and tightened it after. It is the centerpiece of the product.

  1. I.

    Every cited case must exist in our verified corpus.

    If the model attempts to return a case our corpus does not contain, the system silently drops it before it reaches you. Hallucinated citations are not a possibility we paper over with a disclaimer.

  2. II.

    Every quoted phrase must appear verbatim in the source.

    A character-for-character substring check. If the quote does not survive verification, the output is regenerated or routed to editor review. You never see an unverified quote.

  3. III.

    Every paragraph anchor must point to a real paragraph.

    The anchor renders as a click-through to the actual passage in the actual judgment. The lawyer can verify in one second. We assume the lawyer will.

  4. IV.

    Every IPC reference is auto-mapped to BNS.

    A maintained graph of IPC ↔ BNS, CrPC ↔ BNSS, and Evidence Act ↔ BSA, surfaced as a one-click bridge on every result for matters arising after 1 July 2024.

Practitioner questions

What lawyers ask first.

Will it hallucinate citations?

By architecture, no. Every cited case must exist in our verified corpus; hallucinations are dropped before they reach you. Every paragraph anchor and quoted phrase is verified against source. After February 2026, we treat this as a non-negotiable.

How current is the corpus?

The current beta corpus is 42 hand-curated landmark Supreme Court judgments. The next milestone is 500 by end of Q2 2026, then 5,000 by Q3 — including post-July-2024 BNS-era rulings. You can request specific cases be added.

Why criminal law only?

Depth, not breadth. Horizontal AI legal-tech is a kilometre wide and a centimetre deep on every domain. Criminal law has its own taxonomy, its own appellate doctrines, its own statutes. We chose to be world-class on one thing rather than mediocre across all.

Does it work in Hindi?

Yes. Every result has a one-click Hindi toggle. Translation is natural lawyer-Hindi; case names, citations, statute references and paragraph anchors are preserved verbatim in English — those are the things you copy into court papers.

Can I cite Headnote in court?

You cite the underlying judgment, not Headnote. Every result links you to the source paragraph. Treat Headnote as a research assistant, not a citation source.

Who is behind this?

A small founding team working with practising criminal advocates. Editorial supervision comes from a senior advocate with twenty-six years at the Bar. We do not publish individual names while in beta.
