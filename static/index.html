About this tool

Criminal Law AI is a research prototype for Indian criminal-law practitioners. It produces journal-format headnotes (Cri.L.J. style) and practitioner notes (chambers digest style) for Indian judgments, and matches lawyer situations against a curated corpus of landmark cases.

Built around three principles

  1. Hallucination-proof by architecture. Every cited case is verified against a real corpus. The model cannot invent precedents.
  2. Two output styles. Formal journal headnote for citation; compressed chambers notes for working files.
  3. BNS/BNSS aware. Every IPC/CrPC reference is mapped to its post-1-July-2024 successor.

Current limitations

  • Corpus is 42 cases (v0). Production target: 50,000+.
  • No PDF upload yet — paste text only.
  • Always verify citations in the source judgment before relying in court. After the SC's Feb 2026 ruling on AI hallucinations as misconduct, this is mandatory.
