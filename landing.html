How research happens, end-to-end

See the work, shipped.

Three ways lawyers use Headnote depending on where they are in a matter — from the first situation brief to the final draft submission.

01

Describe a situation. Get matched cases.

Type the matter in plain English — "client charged with cheating, civil dispute, no dishonest intent at inception." Headnote returns the 3–5 most relevant precedents from a curated corpus, each with a journal headnote and a "why this matches" explanation. 

situation → 3-5 cases
style     → journal | practitioner
verified  → every case_id, every quote
02

Ask a topic. Get a chambers digest.

For doctrinal research — "five-fold test for circumstantial evidence", "S. 482 quashing on settlement". Headnote produces a topic-organised research digest grouping cases under sub-topic headings, the way senior advocates' associates write notebooks. 

topic     → notebook digest
grouped   → by sub-issue
closes    → with takeaway
03

Paste a judgment. Get a headnote.

Drop full judgment text. Headnote produces lettered headnotes — one per discrete point of law — in both Cri.L.J. journal format and parallel practitioner notes. Plus the cases referred, with treatment classification (followed, distinguished, overruled, referred). 

judgment  → (A) (B) (C)…
format    → journal + practitioner
extras    → cases referred + treatment

FAQ

Things lawyers ask.

Will it hallucinate citations?

By architecture, no. Every cited case must exist in our verified corpus; if Headnote tries to return a case not in the corpus, the system drops it before it reaches you. Every quoted phrase is checked against the source paragraph. After the SC's February 2026 ruling, this is the bar.

How current is the corpus?

The current beta corpus covers landmark Supreme Court rulings up to early 2025 and a curated set of recent High Court rulings. Corpus expansion to 5,000+ cases — including post-July-2024 BNS-era judgments — is the next milestone. Lawyers can request specific cases be added; we ingest them.

Why is it Indian-criminal-law-only?

Depth, not breadth. Horizontal legal AI is shallow on every domain. Criminal law has its own taxonomy, its own statutes, its own appellate doctrines. We chose to be world-class on one thing rather than mediocre across all.

Does it work in Hindi?

Every output has a one-click Hindi toggle. Translation is natural, lawyer-appropriate Devanagari. Case names, citations, statute names, and paragraph anchors are preserved verbatim in English — those are the things you copy into court papers.

Can I cite Headnote's output in court?

You cite the underlying judgment, not Headnote. Every result links you to the source paragraph in the actual judgment so you can read and quote it directly. Treat Headnote as a research assistant, not as a citation source.

What is "BNS / BNSS / BSA" awareness?

On 1 July 2024, India's IPC, CrPC, and Evidence Act were repealed and replaced by the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, and Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam respectively. Headnote auto-maps every reference between the old and new statutes so a search for an IPC section also surfaces BNS-era precedents.
